THE 10 INDICATORS

What Vexeom measures and why it compounds.

01
Recovery
Physical restoration and adaptation rate
Recovery is not rest. It is the rate at which the body and nervous system return to baseline after sustained demand. Candidates who cannot recover between cycles accumulate deficits that compound across every other indicator.
→ Fatigue, Cognitive Load, Career Sustainability
02
Fatigue
Cumulative load and operational reserve
EASA FTL 2024 linked fatigue to 23% of reported EU operator incidents. Fatigue in training presents as slower processing and narrowed situational awareness — by the time it is noticed, it has already compounded into other indicators.
→ Cognitive Load, Training Readiness, Career Sustainability
03
Cognitive Load
Focus reliability under sustained demand
82% of EU/UK airlines deploy AI-assisted cognitive assessment at selection. Candidates who have never tracked their cognitive capacity during training arrive at these systems without a baseline. The AI finds what you have not measured. Vexeom measures it first.
→ Training Readiness, Stress, Career Sustainability
04
Stress
Load, adaptability, and pressure tolerance
The training environment is deliberately stressful. What differentiates candidates who survive from those who do not is not the absence of stress but the presence of adaptive capacity calibrated for a sustained, high-demand career.
→ Recovery, Motivation Stability, Lifestyle Compatibility
05
Circadian Stability
Sleep timing and schedule resilience
Commercial aviation is a 24-hour industry. EASA FTL now legally requires operators to manage circadian disruption. Pilots who have never stress-tested their circadian resilience will not know their vulnerability until the roster reveals it.
→ Recovery, Fatigue, Cognitive Load
06
Training Readiness
Study discipline and self-management
ICAO 2025 mandates Human Factors as a core type rating component. Self-management is now formally assessed. Candidates without a documented history of self-managed training are entering an assessed environment without the evidence base it requires.
→ Cognitive Load, Career Sustainability, Motivation Stability
07
Lifestyle Compatibility
Environmental stability and schedule flexibility
CAA 2024 formally identifies relationship instability as a primary stressor that compounds fatigue and reduces cognitive reserve. Most candidates assess technical readiness exhaustively. Almost none formally assess environmental readiness. The two are not interchangeable.
→ Stress, Recovery, Motivation Stability
08
Financial Resilience
Economic stability under training cost pressure
The average UK integrated ATPL exceeds £150,000. Financial pressure is the most commonly cited factor in voluntary training withdrawal — ahead of washout and medical failure combined. It is the most predictable and the most preventable failure trigger.
→ Stress, Fatigue, Motivation Stability
09
Motivation Stability
Commitment consistency and career conviction
Boeing 2024 identifies motivation instability as the second most common non-technical cause of training failure globally. Motivation does not collapse suddenly — it erodes progressively, compounding with stress and financial pressure until withdrawal becomes rational.
→ Training Readiness, Career Sustainability, Lifestyle Compatibility
10
Career Sustainability
Long-range trajectory and systemic resilience
The terminal indicator. Career sustainability reflects the aggregate state of all nine upstream indicators weighted against each other. A high score means the system is holding. A low score means something upstream has already begun to fail.
